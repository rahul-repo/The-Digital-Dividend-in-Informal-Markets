# Digital Dividend in Informal Markets: UPI Adoption Analysis

## Overview

This project studies the impact of digital payment adoption (UPI intensity) on informal-sector vendor outcomes using survey-based data. The analysis focuses on understanding how digital transaction systems influence revenue stability, payment behavior, and vendor perceptions in informal markets.

---

## Project Year: 2026

## Objectives

* Construct a UPI intensity index from survey responses.
* Analyze the relationship between digital payment usage and vendor outcomes.
* Examine revenue instability and sales loss due to cash shortages.
* Study behavioral and perceptual aspects of digital payment adoption.

---

## Data

The study uses structured survey data collected from informal-sector vendors. The dataset includes variables related to payment modes, transaction behavior, revenue patterns, and vendor perceptions of digital payment systems.

---

## Methodology

### Exploratory Analysis

* Data cleaning and preprocessing
* Descriptive statistics
* Visualization of vendor characteristics

### Statistical Inference

* Pearson’s Chi-square test for categorical associations
* Fisher’s exact test for small-sample inference
* Mann–Whitney U test for non-parametric comparison

### Regression and Modeling

* Logistic regression for payment behavior analysis
* Multinomial logistic regression for categorical outcomes
* Bayesian estimation for revenue instability modeling

### Index Construction

* UPI intensity index developed from survey indicators

---

## Key Analyses

* Effect of UPI intensity on revenue stability
* Impact of digital payments on sales loss due to cash constraints
* Vendor perception analysis (trust, speed, reliability, tracking)
* Behavioral segmentation using regression and Bayesian methods
